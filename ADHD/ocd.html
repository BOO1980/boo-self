OCD and ADHD

  • OCD and ADHD are distinct but also comorbid disorders that can share similar symptoms, behaviours and outcomes
  • This overlap can complicate diagosis and treatment for patients with both conditions

What is OCD

  • OCD is characterized by having obsessions and/or compulsions
  • Obsessions can include recurrent, persistent thoughts, visions and impulses that are experienced as intrusive and inappropriate
  • These thoughts cause anxiety and distress to the individual
  • Thoughts, impulses and mental images are not simply excessive worries about real-life problems
  • The individual often attemps to ignore or surpress such thoughts, impulses, or images or to neutralize them with some other thought or action
  • The individual recongnises that obsessive thoughts, impulsesm or images are a product of their own mind
  • Compulsions can include repetitive behaviours or mental acts that the indivudal feels driven to perform in response to a obsession, or according to rules that must be applied rigidly
  • The repetitive behavours or mental acts are aimed at preventing or reducing distress, or at preventing some dreaded event or situation
  • However, these behaviours or mental acts either are not connected in a realistic way with what they are designed to neutralize or prevent, or they are clearly excessive

    OCD and executive functions

    • OCD is also an exective functioning disorder
    • When an individual has obsessive thoughts and compulsions, it leads to executive overload, leaving litte toom to attend anything else
    • individuals with OCD experience the following:
      • Diffculty with planning and with working memory deficts, due to trouble filtering out irrelevant data while feeding a need for completeness
      • Selective attention deficit to what is threatening or distressing. With ADHD, attention is directed to what is simulating
      • impared inhibition as an element of compulsivity (inhibition: a feeling that makes ones self-conscious and unable to act in a relaxed and natural way)
      • Decicion: making problems due to excessive over-thinking, compared to individuas with ADHD who may be more impulsive
      • An inflated sense of responsibility and perfectionism, which can make taking actions and making decisions much more profound then they need to be

    Why ADHD is missed in OCD Patients

    • ADHD and OCD symptoms can intertwine, even though the basis and motivation for the behaviours may differ
    • Percieved compulsions might be based in ADHD more than OCD as individuals with ADHD often have to create specific environments and structures to function.
    • Sensory sensitivity and defensiveness could be mistaken for perfectionist tendencies
