OCD and ADHD

  • OCD and ADHD are distinct but also comorbid disorders that can share similar symptoms, behaviours and outcomes
  • This overlap can complicate diagosis and treatment for patients with both conditions

What is OCD

  • OCD is characterized by having obsessions and/or compulsions
  • Obsessions can include recurrent, persistent thoughts, visions and impulses that are experienced as intrusive and inappropriate
  • These thoughts cause anxiety and distress to the individual
  • Thoughts, impulses and mental images are not simply excessive worries about real-life problems
  • The individual often attemps to ignore or surpress such thoughts, impulses, or images or to neutralize them with some other thought or action
  • The individual recongnises that obsessive thoughts, impulsesm or images are a product of their own mind
  • Compulsions can include repetitive behaviours or mental acts that the indivudal feels driven to perform in response to a obsession, or according to rules that must be applied rigidly
  • The repetitive behavours or mental acts are aimed at preventing or reducing distress, or at preventing some dreaded event or situation
  • However, these behaviours or mental acts either are not connected in a realistic way with what they are designed to neutralize or prevent, or they are clearly excessive
