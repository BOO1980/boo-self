ADHD Symptoms

  • Short attention span, especially for non-preferred tasks
  • Hyperactivity, which may be physical, verbal and/or emotional
  • Impulsivity, which may manifest as recklessness
  • Fidgeting or restlesness
  • Disorganisation and diffculty priortising tasks
  • Frequent mood swings and emotional dysregulation
  • Forgetfulness and poor working memory
  • Trouble multitasking and exective dysfunctions
  • Inability to control anger or frustration
  • Trouble completing tasks and frequent procrastiations
  • Distractibility
  • Difficulty awaiting turn

Different types of ADHD

  • ADHD is a neurological condition defined by a consistant pattern or inattention and/or hyperactive impulsivity that interferes with daily functioning
  • ADHD comprises of three subtypes:
    • inattentive type
    • hyperactive of impulsive type
    • combined type

Inattentive ADD checklist

  • Fails to give close attention to details or make careless mistakes in work
  • Had difficulty sustaining attention in tasks
  • Does not seem to listen when spoken directly
  • Has difficulty organising tasks and activiies
  • Loses things necessary for tasks or acticvities
  • Easily distracted by extraneous stimuli
  • Forgetful in daily activities

Hyperactive and impulsive

  • Fidgets with hands or feet or squirms in seat
  • Leaves seat in classroom or in other situations in which remaining seated is expected
  • Runs about or climbs excessively in situations in which it is inappropriate
  • Has difficult playing or engaging in leisure activities quietly
  • Appears on the go or acts as driven by a motor
  • Blurts out the answers before questions have been completed
  • Interrupts or intrudes on others (butts in coversations or games)
